Research Interest

  My research interests lie in Knowledge Tracing — modeling the evolving knowledge states of learners through their problem-solving histories, and leveraging these representations for downstream tasks such as problem recommender systems. My current focus is on developing a knowledge tracing model that moves beyond conventional learner modeling approaches, which have largely relied on correct/incorrect response sequences, by integrating students' problem-solving process data to model their evolving knowledge states in a more fine-grained and cognitively grounded manner. Furthermore, I am also interested in extending modeling techniques that reflect complex cognitive processes based on multimodal data to recommender systems in other domains.
